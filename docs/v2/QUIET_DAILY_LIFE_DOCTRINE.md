# Quiet Daily Life Doctrine

**Program ID:** `BLOCK-STREET-QUIET-DAILY-LIFE-1.0` · **Requirement prefix:** `QDL-*`  
**Accepted:** 2026-07-16  
**Status:** Canonical · governing product principle  
**Machine twin:** `data/registry/quiet-daily-life-doctrine.json`

> **The system may be complex underneath, but it must feel calm, personal, and almost obvious to the person using it.**

## QDL-001 — Governing Principle

**[QDL-001]** Block-Street should feel less like campaign software and more like a familiar local place people visit every day.

**[QDL-001a]** Authority, scope resolution, mutation coverage, shadow parity, CPOS doctrine, and build-stage language belong in **Director, Operator, Architect, and engineering views** — not volunteer-facing screens.

**[QDL-001b]** Design for **meaningful return**, not compulsive return. Avoid gamification that makes civic participation feel childish.

**[QDL-001c]** Progressive disclosure is the interface principle: show the essential action first; defer advanced information until the user asks or grows into it.

---

## QDL-002 — Product Atmosphere

**[QDL-002]** Target atmosphere:

```text
Quiet · Personal · Useful · Local · Welcoming · Unhurried · Easy to understand · Alive without feeling noisy
```

**[QDL-002a]** “Underwhelming” in the best sense:

- No giant dashboards on first login
- No walls of metrics
- No dense navigation
- No unnecessary explanations
- No technical status banners
- No twelve competing calls to action
- No algorithmic social-media chaos

**[QDL-002b]** Complexity appears only when the person needs it.

---

## QDL-003 — Psychological Model

**[QDL-003]** Every volunteer-facing surface should support three human needs (Self-Determination Theory):

| Need | User should feel | System provides |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Autonomy** | “I choose how I participate.” | Cause selection · availability control · communication preferences · gradual project join · home rearrangement · decline without punishment · visibility controls |
| **Competence** | “I understand what to do, and I can see myself getting better.” | One clear next action · plain instructions · embedded training · visible completion · helpful feedback · expanding responsibility · clear block explanations |
| **Relatedness** | “I belong here, and people know me.” | Local connections · team membership · real recognition · shared ideas · visible community discussion · friends · county/campus identity · nearby help opportunities |

## QDL-004 — Daily-Use Loop (My Home)

**[QDL-004]** On open, every user should immediately understand:

```text
What is happening?
Is there anything I need to do?
What are people near me talking about?
How can I participate?
```

**[QDL-004a]** **My Home** contains at most five primary elements for most people:

| # | Element | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Today** | One useful action (confirm meeting · welcome someone · share event · add idea · complete contact) |
| 2 | **My Community** | Small stream from committee · county · college/school · friends |
| 3 | **What's Coming Up** | Next meeting · nearby event · assignment · deadline |
| 4 | **My People** | Friends · team · leaders · new joiners · people invited |
| 5 | **Explore** | Quiet invitation to expand (help · county info · project · learn) |

**[QDL-004b]** Mobile primary nav aligns to: **Today · Community · Calendar · People · Me**.

## QDL-005 — Growth Stages

**[QDL-005]** The application grows with the person; do not give every new volunteer every module.

| Stage | Who | Surfaces |
|-------|-----|----------|
| **1 — New arrival** | First days | Welcome · My place · My next step · My team · Upcoming meeting |
| **2 — Connected member** | Joined committee or connections | Community board · Friends · Ideas · Events · Simple task list · Learning |
| **3 — Active volunteer** | Taking part | Assignments · Projects · Calendar · Progress · Volunteer activity · Additional committees |
| **4 — Leader** | Earned leadership | People management · Goals · Reports · Planning · Approvals · Project board · County/campus intelligence |
| **5 — Senior leadership** | Campaign command | Cross-command dependencies · County/cluster rollups · Project portfolios · Election-plan progress · Operational risks |

**[QDL-005a]** Stage gates are **behavior- and role-based**, not time-based. Same product, expanding projection.

## QDL-006 — Personal Board

**[QDL-006]** Each user keeps a **personal board** on their dashboard — a calm home, not a metrics wall.

**[QDL-006a]** User-controlled elements include:

- **Availability calendar** — when they can help, travel, or need quiet weeks
- What they care about · how they like to help
- Current projects and public contributions
- Locations (county · campus · places they serve)
- Committee work and upcoming shared events

**[QDL-006b]** Profile design (human, not surveillance):

```text
Photo · Name · County or community · College/school when applicable
What I care about · How I like to help · Current projects
Public contributions · Friends · Upcoming shared events
```

**[QDL-006c]** **Never display** on ordinary profiles: sensitive political targeting · voter history · private contact information · administrative status · unnecessary activity surveillance.

**[QDL-006d]** Aligns with [Personal Headquarters](../phase-03/PARTICIPANT_PROFILE_SYSTEM.md) registry — terminology stays personal, not “user dashboard.”

## QDL-007 — Manager People Board

**[QDL-007]** Upper-level managers (county lead · college lead · volunteer manager · campaign director) open a **person board** when they click on someone.

**[QDL-007a]** Leader view shows only what coordination requires:

- What they are working on · interests · **availability**
- Locations · committee work · team membership
- Shared events · recent public contributions · connection context

**[QDL-007b]** Leader view must **not** expose: engineering authority state · certification internals · shadow-mode diagnostics · unrelated statewide personnel unless the viewer holds that band ([Audience Sectioning](./AUDIENCE_SECTIONING_DOCTRINE.md)).

**[QDL-007c]** One person object — many projections ([QDL-011 Rule 6](#qdl-011--simplification-rules)).

---

## QDL-008 — Friends and Trusted Connections

**[QDL-008]** Connections mean: *people I know, trust, work with, or want to stay connected to in this movement* — not social competition.

**[QDL-008a]** Supported:

- Connection request · accept/decline · shared committees and places
- Public contributions · mentions on board posts
- Invite to events · projects · recognize contribution · small working groups

**[QDL-008b]** **Do not emphasize:** follower counts · popularity · public connection totals · viral content · engagement rankings · influencer status.

---

## QDL-009 — Open Community Board

**[QDL-009]** Primary communication is **open within the appropriate community scope** — not hidden direct-message culture.

**[QDL-009a]** Each space gets a board: committee · college · county · cluster · campaign-wide · project-specific.

**[QDL-009b]** Post types: Idea · Question · Announcement · Request for help · Event · Update · Resource · Recognition · Decision · Poll.

**[QDL-009c]** Interactions: Reply · Mention · Acknowledge · Follow · Save · Volunteer · Add to project · Report concern.

**[QDL-009d]** Every post states visibility clearly:

```text
Visible to: Clark County members
Posted by: Jordan Smith · Clark County Outreach
Related to: March voter-registration project
```

**[QDL-009e]** **Initial release:** scoped boards · threaded replies · mentions · moderation · reporting · **no private messaging**.

**[QDL-009f]** **Later (operational only):** limited direct contact for leader↔assigned volunteer · event lead↔shift · outreach↔recruit · safety/admin — tied to workflow, retained, reportable.

**[QDL-009g]** Feed ordering — transparent rules, no opaque engagement algorithm:

1. Pinned leadership information  
2. Assignments and mentions  
3. Your committee  
4. Your local place  
5. Friends and followed projects  
6. Campaign-wide information  

User filters: **For me · My community · Latest · Ideas · Needs help · Events**

## QDL-010 — Ideas Lifecycle

**[QDL-010]** Ideas are a unifying feature connecting board → projects → tasks → events.

**[QDL-010a]** Lifecycle (visible to submitter):

```text
Shared → Discussing → Needs information → Under review → Added to project
→ In progress → Completed → Not moving forward (with explanation)
```

**[QDL-010b]** Convertible into: project · task · event · poll · community meeting · training lesson · request to county leader.

**[QDL-010c]** Connects to Assistant Campaign Manager / Asana-style project system — one object, many projections.

---

## QDL-011 — Simplification Rules

**[QDL-011]** Non-negotiable product rules:

| # | Rule |
|---|------|
| 1 | **One primary action** per page — most likely next step unmistakable |
| 2 | **Plain language** — “Your next meeting” not “Operational readiness dimension” |
| 3 | **Hide system truth** from ordinary users — no build status, phase numbers, route names, DB mode, certification jargon |
| 4 | **Reveal on demand** — card shows title · one-line meaning · status · next action; then “View details” |
| 5 | **No empty complexity** — hide unused modules; show helpful empty states |
| 6 | **One object, many projections** — a task on five workbenches is one task |
| 7 | **Mobile is primary** for the everyday loop |

**[QDL-011a]** Service messages when backend state affects the user:

| Instead of | Show |
|------------|------|
| Postgres authority is operating in shadow mode. | Some administrative changes may take longer to appear. |

## QDL-012 — Embedded Training

**[QDL-012]** Training is everywhere but rarely occupies the screen unless requested.

**[QDL-012a]** Universal label: **Learn how** — one consistent treatment, not random bold/italic conventions.

**[QDL-012b]** Drawer contents: What this does · Three steps · Why it matters · See full lesson.

**[QDL-012c]** Progression: Quick answer → short guide → full lesson → demonstration → practice → certification (where required).

**[QDL-012d]** Training follows the current task — not a giant manual search.

## QDL-013 — County Workbench Bridge

**[QDL-013]** Integration path:

```text
RedDirt County Workbench
        ↓
Shared county intelligence contract
        ↓
Block-Street county and college spaces
```

**[QDL-013a]** **County members** see: events · volunteer opportunities · projects · ideas · organizations · basic public facts · registration progress (where appropriate) · people/teams · training.

**[QDL-013b]** **County leadership** additionally see: election-plan goals · registration targets · contact progress · volunteer strength · leadership vacancies · risks · cluster comparisons · approved aggregates.

**[QDL-013c]** **Campaign leadership** see: all county workbenches · cluster rollups · goal pace · volunteer growth · data confidence.

---

## QDL-014 — College ↔ County Reciprocal Views

**[QDL-014]** Every college space knows its county (institution · county · cluster).

**[QDL-014a]** College receives county context: events · registration goals · organizations · projects · demographics/workforce · county leadership · shared volunteer needs.

**[QDL-014b]** County receives college attribution: campus volunteers · registration · events · projects · leadership · student outreach · local ideas.

**[QDL-014c]** One relationship, not copied information:

```text
College activity → attributed to institution → rolls into county → cluster → campaign
```

---

## QDL-016 — Daily-Use Certification

**[QDL-016]** A regular user should pass this test without human help:

```text
Open the app · Understand today · See their community · Respond to someone
Join an activity · Find help · Learn how · Return tomorrow
```

**[QDL-016a]** Measure: time to understand home · time to first meaningful action · human-help count · unnecessary clicks · abandoned paths · repeat voluntary visits · felt belonging.

**[QDL-016b]** Avoid arbitrary streaks that punish people for having lives.

---

## QDL-017 — North-Star Experience

**[QDL-017a]** **Regular volunteer:**

> “This is my community. These are my people. Here is what is happening. Here is one way I can help.”

**[QDL-017b]** **County commander:**

> “I understand my county. I know what needs attention. I can reach the right people. I know what comes next.”

**[QDL-017c]** **Campaign manager:**

> “The movement is visible without being overwhelming.”

## Evaluation Checklist

Before shipping any volunteer-facing change, answer:

1. Does this feel **calm and personal** ([QDL-001])?
2. Is there **one primary action** ([QDL-011])?
3. Is copy **plain language** with no authority/engineering terms ([QDL-015])?
4. Is the **audience band** correct ([Audience Sectioning](./AUDIENCE_SECTIONING_DOCTRINE.md))?
5. Does it support **autonomy, competence, or relatedness** ([QDL-003])?
6. Would a new volunteer understand it in **under 10 seconds** ([QDL-016])?
