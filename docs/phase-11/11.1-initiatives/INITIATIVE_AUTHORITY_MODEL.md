# Initiative Authority Model

**Build:** 11.1 · **Wave:** W1 · **Canonical source:** [01_CONSTITUTION.md](01_CONSTITUTION.md)

## Human Ownership

### Executive Owner
- Authorizes initiative strategically
- Secures institutional support
- Resolves major escalations
- Accepts closeout

### Operational Owner
- Maintains charter and coordinates workstreams
- Tracks progress and blockers
- Prepares decisions and reporting
- Initiates closeout

### Backup Owner
Strongly recommended for: high-risk initiatives, long-running programs, emergency operations, public deadlines, youth involvement, financial responsibility.

## Ownership Eligibility

Owner must be: canonical Human, active institution membership (or approved participating role), appropriate authority, not restricted, with accepted assignment.

## Ownership Prohibitions

Anonymous · service identity · generic department account · shared login · AI · deleted/inactive Human · unapproved external partner · automation process.

## Authority Matrix (Summary)

| Role | May | May Not |
|------|-----|---------|
| Draft Creator | Create draft, propose scope, nominate owners | Activate, allocate restricted resources, send official communications |
| Operational Owner | Maintain charter, coordinate preparation, report, propose changes | Exceed institutional authority |
| Executive Owner | Sponsor approval, strategic change, closeout acceptance | Bypass board/legal/coalition/safety approval |
| Initiative Administrator | Technical configuration, support, authorized metadata repair | Invent authority, approve strategy, change scope unauthorized |
| Institution Executive | Portfolio view, policy-based approval, institutional support | Bypass required approvals |

## Owner Required State

When operational owner becomes ineligible:
1. Enter `owner_required`
2. Pause high-risk new execution
3. Notify executive owner
4. Create operations work item
5. Require reassignment
6. Preserve records

Enforcement: 11.1-W3 service, 11.1-W7 operations queue.

## Emergency Authority

Expedited activation permitted with recorded: Human authority, reason, scope, start, expiration, temporary powers, review deadline, after-action requirement.

Emergency status must not become permanent (CON-018, SEC-006).
