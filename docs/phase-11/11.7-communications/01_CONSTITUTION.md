Establish the constitutional foundation governing every conversation, discussion, announcement, meeting, decision, document, and collaboration event within the Institution.

Build 11.1 defined institutional purpose.

Build 11.2 defined objectives.

Build 11.3 defined operational execution.

Communication is no longer an external activity.

## Constitutional Doctrine

Communication exists to improve execution.

Every message should answer one or more questions:

* What happened?
* What is happening?
* What decision was made?
* What action is required?
* Who needs to know?
* What did we learn?

The platform discourages noise.

It encourages clarity.

---

## Communication Philosophy

Communication is not entertainment.

Communication is institutional infrastructure.

The platform values:

* clarity
* accountability
* permanence
* transparency
* collaboration
* respect
* learning

Conversation should reduce uncertainty.

---

## Constitutional Principles

### Principle 1

Every communication belongs somewhere.

A communication should be attached to:

* Institution
* Initiative
* Objective
* Mission
* Meeting
* Decision
* Team
* Organization

Or another constitutional object.

Orphan conversations are prohibited.

---

### Principle 2

Identity is always verified.

Every message is attributable to a verified Human.

Anonymous institutional communication does not exist.

---

### Principle 3

Communication is permanent institutional knowledge.

Messages may become historical.

They are not forgotten.

Institutional memory survives personnel changes.

---

### Principle 4

Operational conversations belong with operational work.

Mission discussions remain attached to Missions.

Objective discussions remain attached to Objectives.

Meeting notes remain attached to Meetings.

Decisions remain attached to Decisions.

Context is never separated from execution.

---

### Principle 5

Communication should reduce meetings.

If information can be documented once and referenced repeatedly, that is preferred over repeating it in multiple meetings.
