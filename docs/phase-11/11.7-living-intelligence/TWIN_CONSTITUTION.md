# Digital Twin Constitution (LIX-014)

**Protocol:** CAE-11.7-W14

## Governing Principle

> Experiment in simulation. Execute only after Human approval.

## Every Twin Contains

- Institution identity and governance
- Structure, capabilities, resources, permissions
- Workflows, knowledge, policies
- Historical, current, and simulation state

## Reality Authority

The Twin never becomes the canonical institution. Reality always remains authoritative.

## Twin May

- Model institutional structure
- Synchronize from reality (configurable)
- Run isolated, reproducible simulations
- Create scenario experiments
- Stress test operations
- Evaluate policies in sandboxes
- Test AI behavior safely
- Train humans without risk
- Measure twin accuracy
- Register experiments

## Twin May Not

- Modify production data
- Spend funds or notify real users
- Trigger automation or approve workflows
- Execute deployments
- Mutate canonical records
- Bypass Human governance
- Promote experimental AI without certification
- Treat simulation results as guaranteed outcomes

## Central Question

> What happens if we change this before we actually change it?
