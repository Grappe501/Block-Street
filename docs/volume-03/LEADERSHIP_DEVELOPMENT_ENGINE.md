## Purpose

**[PBA-LDE01]** The Leadership Development Engine defines how the Community Operating System **identifies, develops, mentors, equips, supports, transitions, and preserves community leadership**.

**[PBA-LDE01a]** Leadership is **not a position** — it is a lifelong journey of service, stewardship, and multiplication.

**[PBA-LDE01b]** The purpose is not simply to fill leadership roles — it is to **continuously build the next generation of community builders**.

---

## Guiding Principle

**[PBA-LDE02]**

> **The measure of a leader is the leaders they develop.**

**[PBA-LDE02a]** Every leadership pathway should produce **more capable leaders than it began with**.

---

## Philosophy

**[PBA-LDE03]** Traditional leadership systems ask: Who is in charge?

**[PBA-LDE03a]** The Community Operating System asks:

- Who is growing?
- Who is ready?
- Who needs mentoring?
- Who is overextended?
- Who should succeed the current leader?
- How do we preserve institutional knowledge?

**[PBA-LDE03b]** **Leadership becomes a renewable community resource.**

---

## Leadership Lifecycle

**[PBA-LDE04]** Every leader progresses through a structured lifecycle:

```text
Potential
      ↓
Discovery
      ↓
Preparation
      ↓
Service
      ↓
Leadership
      ↓
Mentorship
      ↓
Stewardship
      ↓
Succession
      ↓
Legacy
```

**[PBA-LDE04a]** Leadership is **developmental rather than positional**.

---

## Lifecycle Stages

**[PBA-LDE05]** Nine lifecycle stages:

### Stage 1 — Potential

**[PBA-LDE05a]** Potential may be identified through:

Volunteer participation · Community recommendations · Mentor observations · Mission contributions · Knowledge sharing · Service consistency

**Leadership begins with service.**

### Stage 2 — Discovery

**[PBA-LDE05b]** Potential leaders explore:

Leadership pathways · Responsibilities · Community needs · Training · Mentorship opportunities

**Discovery remains voluntary.**

### Stage 3 — Preparation

**[PBA-LDE05c]** Preparation includes:

Training · Shadowing · Committee participation · Mission leadership · Leadership simulations · Knowledge development

**Preparation reduces future failure.**

### Stage 4 — Service

**[PBA-LDE05d]** Leadership begins with active service:

Committee leadership · Mission coordination · Volunteer mentoring · Knowledge stewardship · Community facilitation

**Leadership is practiced before titles.**

### Stage 5 — Leadership

**[PBA-LDE05e]** Formal leadership responsibilities include:

Community organization · Mission oversight · Volunteer support · Strategic planning · Partnership development · Community care

**Leadership remains accountable.**

### Stage 6 — Mentorship

**[PBA-LDE05f]** Leaders intentionally develop:

Future leaders · Committee chairs · Mission coordinators · Volunteers · Community organizers

**Leadership multiplies itself.**

### Stage 7 — Stewardship

**[PBA-LDE05g]** Experienced leaders become stewards of:

Culture · Knowledge · Relationships · Traditions · Community history · Institutional memory

**Stewardship protects continuity.**

### Stage 8 — Succession

**[PBA-LDE05h]** Succession includes:

Successor identification · Knowledge transfer · Mentorship overlap · Leadership transition · Celebration · Historical preservation

**Succession is planned rather than reactive** [PBA-CLE06i · Community Lifecycle].

### Stage 9 — Legacy

**[PBA-LDE05i]** Legacy preserves:

Stories · Leadership history · Playbooks · Lessons · Community traditions · Mission impact

**Legacy becomes community memory.**

## Major Architectural Recommendation: Leadership Academy

**[PBA-LDE18]** Introduce a **Leadership Academy** as the operational environment for developing community leaders.

**[PBA-LDE18a]** The Leadership Academy is **not simply a training catalog** — it is a **living leadership development system** that adapts to each participant's journey.

**[PBA-LDE18b]** The Academy brings together eight domains:

| Domain | Brings Together |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Leadership Journey** | Current stage · Growth pathway · Goals · Milestones |
| **Learning** | Courses · Workshops · Reading · Simulations · Reflection exercises |
| **Mentorship** | Assigned mentor · Mentorship history · Coaching sessions · Feedback · Successor planning |
| **Leadership Practice** | Missions led · Committees served · Volunteer development · Community facilitation · Strategic planning |
| **Community Intelligence** | Community Health · Leadership pipeline · Succession readiness · Capacity gaps |
| **Knowledge** | Leadership playbooks · Stories · Lessons learned · Historical case studies · Community Brain |
| **Personal Reflection** | Leadership journal · Goals · Strengths · Growth opportunities · AI coaching summaries |
| **Recognition** | Service anniversaries · Milestones · Certifications · Community acknowledgments |

**[PBA-LDE18c]** The Leadership Academy becomes the platform where **future organizers, mentors, committee chairs, and statewide leaders are intentionally developed**.

**[PBA-LDE18d]** If the Personal Operating System supports the individual, the Volunteer Success Center supports service, the Mission Operations Center supports execution, and the Community Operating Manual supports organizations, then the **Leadership Academy supports the people who will sustain and grow those organizations over generations**.

**[PBA-LDE18e]** The platform's greatest product is **not software — it is capable community leaders**.

**[PBA-LDE18f]** Live spec: `data/registry/leadership-development-engine.json` · `leadershipAcademy`

## Burt Implementation Guidance

**[PBA-LDE19]** Implementation should:

1. Treat leadership as a **developmental journey**
2. **Separate leadership identity from specific offices**
3. Preserve **complete leadership history**
4. Integrate **mentorship into every leadership pathway**
5. Connect leadership with **calendars, knowledge, Digital Twins, and Community Health**
6. Design **succession as a continuous process**
7. Consult **Leadership Academy** spec before leadership-facing development features

**[PBA-LDE19a]** Logical home: Platform Behavior schema — LeadershipDevelopment · LeadershipCompetency · LeadershipPathway · LeadershipPortfolio · LeadershipHealth · LeadershipAcademy · SuccessionPlan.
