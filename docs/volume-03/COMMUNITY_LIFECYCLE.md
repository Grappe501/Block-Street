## Purpose

**[PBA-CLE01]** The Community Lifecycle Engine defines how every community is **proposed, approved, launched, developed, sustained, expanded, renewed, and preserved** throughout its existence.

**[PBA-CLE01a]** A community is **not merely a database record** — it is a living organization with its own identity, relationships, traditions, knowledge, leadership, and institutional memory.

**[PBA-CLE01b]** The Community Lifecycle Engine ensures that communities **mature intentionally** rather than growing accidentally.

---

## Guiding Principle

**[PBA-CLE02]**

> **Communities are cultivated, not simply created.**

**[PBA-CLE02a]** The platform should **support communities throughout their entire life** — not only at launch.

---

## Philosophy

**[PBA-CLE03]** Traditional platforms focus on creating groups.

**[PBA-CLE03a]** The Community Operating System focuses on helping communities **flourish**. The platform should answer:

- Is this community ready to launch?
- Is it healthy?
- Is leadership sustainable?
- Is knowledge being preserved?
- Is the community expanding?
- Is succession prepared?
- Is its legacy protected?

**[PBA-CLE03b]** The lifecycle **extends beyond activation**.

---

## Community Lifecycle

**[PBA-CLE04]** Every community progresses through a structured lifecycle:

```text
Vision
      ↓
Proposal
      ↓
Planning
      ↓
Approval
      ↓
Launch
      ↓
Growth
      ↓
Maturity
      ↓
Expansion
      ↓
Succession
      ↓
Legacy
```

**[PBA-CLE04a]** Communities may move at **different speeds** — stages are not rigid timeboxes.

## Lifecycle Stages

**[PBA-CLE06]** Ten lifecycle stages:

### Stage 1 — Vision

**[PBA-CLE06a]** A need for a community is identified.

Origins: Participant request · Institution request · County initiative · Leadership strategy · Community demand · Growth analysis

**Vision establishes purpose.**

### Stage 2 — Proposal

**[PBA-CLE06b]** A proposal includes:

Purpose · Target institution · Target geography · Expected leadership · Initial goals · Community type · Readiness assessment

**Proposal becomes a governed object** [PBA-WFL06 · Community Workflows].

### Stage 3 — Planning

**[PBA-CLE06c]** Planning includes:

Leadership recruitment · Community charter · Mission definition · Calendar planning · Knowledge space preparation · Communication planning · Partnership identification

**Infrastructure is prepared before launch.**

### Stage 4 — Approval

**[PBA-CLE06d]** Approval evaluates:

Leadership readiness · Community charter · Business rules · Governance · Institutional support · Resource availability

**Approval follows the Workflow Engine** [PBA-003 · Community Process Orchestrator].

### Stage 5 — Launch

**[PBA-CLE06e]** Launch provisions:

Community profile · Leadership assignments · Committee structure · Calendar · Knowledge space · Welcome journey · Initial events · Invitation campaign

**Launch is coordinated rather than manual.**

### Stage 6 — Growth

**[PBA-CLE06f]** Growth includes:

Recruitment · Volunteer development · Knowledge creation · Mission execution · Leadership development · Community traditions

**Growth is measured holistically.**

### Stage 7 — Maturity

**[PBA-CLE06g]** Characteristics include:

Stable leadership · Healthy participation · Knowledge reuse · Community traditions · Mentorship · Operational consistency

**Communities become self-sustaining.**

### Stage 8 — Expansion

**[PBA-CLE06h]** Healthy communities may:

Launch neighboring communities · Support other campuses · Develop regional initiatives · Mentor new leaders · Share playbooks

**Growth becomes network growth.**

### Stage 9 — Succession

**[PBA-CLE06i]** Leadership transitions include:

Mentorship · Knowledge transfer · Historical preservation · Role assignment · Celebration · Continuity planning

**Succession is expected — not exceptional.**

### Stage 10 — Legacy

**[PBA-CLE06j]** Legacy preserves:

Community history · Stories · Traditions · Playbooks · Leadership history · Milestones · Institutional knowledge

**Communities leave lasting impact.**

## Major Architectural Recommendation: Community Operating Manual

**[PBA-CLE18]** Introduce a **Community Operating Manual** for every community.

**[PBA-CLE18a]** Unlike a static handbook, the Operating Manual is a **living document generated from the community's actual activity**.

**[PBA-CLE18b]** The Operating Manual continuously incorporates six domains:

| Domain | Incorporates |
|--------|--------------|
| **Identity** | Mission · Charter · Values · Leadership structure |
| **Operations** | Meeting cadence · Committees · Calendar · Volunteer workflows · Mission templates |
| **Knowledge** | Community Brain · Lessons learned · Playbooks · Stories · Historical milestones |
| **Leadership** | Current leaders · Succession plans · Mentorship pathways · Leadership history |
| **Growth** | Membership trends · Recruitment strategies · Partnerships · Community health indicators |
| **Legacy** | Traditions · Anniversary timeline · Major accomplishments · Community archives |

**[PBA-CLE18c]** Because the manual is **generated from canonical data**, it stays synchronized with the community rather than becoming outdated documentation.

**[PBA-CLE18d]** Every community effectively becomes **self-documenting** — new leaders step into established roles with immediate access to history, operating practices, lessons learned, and future plans.

**[PBA-CLE18e]** As communities mature and mentor new communities, Operating Manuals become **reusable blueprints** that help the statewide network grow consistently while preserving each community's unique culture and traditions.

**[PBA-CLE18f]** Live spec: `data/registry/community-lifecycle-engine.json` · `communityOperatingManual`

## Burt Implementation Guidance

**[PBA-CLE19]** Implementation should:

1. Model communities as **long-lived organizational entities**
2. Preserve **complete lifecycle history**
3. **Separate lifecycle stages from operational status**
4. Integrate with **workflows, calendars, knowledge, and Digital Twins**
5. Preserve **institutional memory after closure**
6. Design communities to **mentor future communities**
7. Consult **Community Operating Manual** spec before community-facing lifecycle features

**[PBA-CLE19a]** Logical home: Platform Behavior schema — CommunityLifecycle · CommunityCharter · CommunityHealth · CommunityCapacity · CommunityOperatingManual · CommunityDigitalTwinProjection.
