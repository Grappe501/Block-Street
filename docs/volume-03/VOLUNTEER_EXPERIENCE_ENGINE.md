## Purpose

**[PBA-VXE01]** The Volunteer Experience Engine defines how people **discover opportunities, become engaged, develop skills, build relationships, assume leadership, and remain connected** throughout their entire journey within the Community Operating System.

**[PBA-VXE01a]** Volunteers are **not a labor pool** — they are **future community leaders**.

**[PBA-VXE01b]** Every interaction should strengthen **belonging, confidence, knowledge, and long-term commitment**.

---

## Guiding Principle

**[PBA-VXE02]**

> **Every volunteer is a future leader, mentor, or community builder.**

**[PBA-VXE02a]** The platform should **nurture people** — not simply assign tasks.

---

## Philosophy

**[PBA-VXE03]** Traditional volunteer systems ask: Who is available? What task needs to be filled?

**[PBA-VXE03a]** The Community Operating System asks:

- What inspires this person?
- Where can they grow?
- Who should mentor them?
- Which community needs their gifts?
- How can this experience become meaningful?
- How do we keep them connected for years?

**[PBA-VXE03b]** **Volunteerism becomes leadership development.**

---

## Volunteer Lifecycle

**[PBA-VXE04]** Every volunteer progresses through a structured experience:

```text
Discovery
      ↓
Interest
      ↓
Conversation
      ↓
Welcome
      ↓
Training
      ↓
Participation
      ↓
Contribution
      ↓
Leadership
      ↓
Mentorship
      ↓
Legacy
```

**[PBA-VXE04a]** **Each stage builds upon the previous.**

---

## Lifecycle Stages

**[PBA-VXE05]** Ten lifecycle stages:

### Stage 1 — Discovery

**[PBA-VXE05a]** Participants discover opportunities through:

Community recommendations · Friends · Events · Institutions · Maps · Search · Discovery Engine · QR codes · Social sharing

**Discovery should feel welcoming rather than transactional** [ENG-010 · DGE-001].

### Stage 2 — Interest

**[PBA-VXE05b]** Participants express interest:

Bookmark opportunity · Request information · Join waiting list · Ask organizer · Subscribe

**Interest is valuable data.**

### Stage 3 — Conversation

**[PBA-VXE05c]** Before assignment, participants may interact with:

Community leaders · Mentors · Organizers · Current volunteers

**Questions build confidence.**

### Stage 4 — Welcome

**[PBA-VXE05d]** Welcome includes:

Orientation · Community introduction · Mission explanation · Calendar · Communication preferences · First connections

**Belonging begins immediately.**

### Stage 5 — Training

**[PBA-VXE05e]** Training may include:

Mission preparation · Safety · Community values · Leadership fundamentals · Operational procedures · Technology orientation

**Training adapts to the volunteer's pathway.**

### Stage 6 — Participation

**[PBA-VXE05f]** Participation records:

Attendance · Volunteer hours · Roles · Contributions · Media · Stories · Relationships

**Participation becomes institutional memory** [DAB-007 · Community Event Ledger].

### Stage 7 — Contribution

**[PBA-VXE05g]** Contribution extends beyond attendance:

Ideas · Leadership · Knowledge · Storytelling · Mentorship assistance · Community improvement

**Contribution strengthens ownership.**

### Stage 8 — Leadership

**[PBA-VXE05h]** Leadership opportunities emerge naturally:

Team lead · Committee member · Organizer · Trainer · Regional coordinator

**Leadership grows from demonstrated service.**

### Stage 9 — Mentorship

**[PBA-VXE05i]** Experienced volunteers support:

New volunteers · Community launches · Leadership development · Knowledge sharing

**Mentorship multiplies capacity.**

### Stage 10 — Legacy

**[PBA-VXE05j]** Long-term contributions become:

Stories · Playbooks · Community traditions · Leadership history · Recognition · Institutional knowledge

**Legacy extends beyond active participation.**

## Major Architectural Recommendation: Volunteer Success Center

**[PBA-VXE19]** Introduce a **Volunteer Success Center (VSC)** as the primary workspace for every participant engaged in service.

**[PBA-VXE19a]** Unlike a simple volunteer dashboard, the Volunteer Success Center **continuously helps participants succeed** throughout their journey.

**[PBA-VXE19b]** The VSC brings together seven domains:

| Domain | Brings Together |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Opportunities** | Recommended volunteer roles · Upcoming missions · Community needs · Nearby events |
| **Commitments** | Calendar · Assignments · Training sessions · Mentorship meetings |
| **Growth** | Skills · Leadership pathway · Learning progress · Certifications · Personal goals |
| **Relationships** | Mentor · Mentees · Teams · Committees · Community leaders |
| **Impact** | Volunteer hours · Missions completed · Stories contributed · Communities served · Recognition earned |
| **Wellness** | Participation balance · Leadership workload · Suggested breaks · Burnout indicators · Celebration reminders |
| **Intelligence** | Volunteer Digital Twin · AI recommendations · Discovery Engine suggestions · Community Health signals |

**[PBA-VXE19c]** The Volunteer Success Center shifts the platform's focus from **"managing volunteers" to helping volunteers thrive**.

**[PBA-VXE19d]** People should feel that the platform is **investing in their growth**, helping them find meaningful opportunities, supporting healthy participation, and preparing them to become the next generation of community leaders.

**[PBA-VXE19e]** Live spec: `data/registry/volunteer-experience-engine.json` · `volunteerSuccessCenter`

## Burt Implementation Guidance

**[PBA-VXE20]** Implementation should:

1. Model volunteering as a **long-term journey**
2. Preserve **complete participation history**
3. **Separate volunteer identity from individual assignments**
4. Integrate with **calendars, knowledge, Digital Twins, and recognition**
5. Treat **mentorship as a natural progression**
6. Support **participant-controlled growth**
7. Consult **Volunteer Success Center** spec before volunteer-facing experience features

**[PBA-VXE20a]** Logical home: Platform Behavior schema — VolunteerExperience · VolunteerProfile · OpportunityMatching · VolunteerJourney · VolunteerWellness · VolunteerSuccessCenter · VolunteerPortfolio.
