## Purpose

**[PBA-OPI01]** The Operational Intelligence Engine serves as the **executive nervous system** of the Community Operating System.

**[PBA-OPI01a]** While the Community Intelligence Engine (Volume 3.8) produces insights within individual domains, the Operational Intelligence Engine **continuously integrates intelligence across the entire platform** to support strategic decision-making.

**[PBA-OPI01b]** It answers the most important executive question:

> **"What deserves our attention right now?"**

## Major Architectural Recommendation: Executive Operations Center

**[PBA-OPI21]** Introduce an **Executive Operations Center (EOC)** as the highest-level operational environment in the Community Operating System.

**[PBA-OPI21a]** The Executive Operations Center is **not a reporting dashboard** — it is the platform's **real-time executive command environment**.

**[PBA-OPI21b]** The EOC continuously integrates every major subsystem across eight networks:

| Network | Integrates |
|---------|------------|
| **Community Network** | Community Health · Growth · Participation · Geographic coverage · Expansion readiness |
| **Leadership Network** | Leadership pipeline · Succession · Mentorship · Workload · Burnout indicators |
| **Mission Network** | Active missions · Risks · Resource allocation · Community impact · Reflection completion |
| **Knowledge Network** | Knowledge Observatory · Community Brain · Playbook adoption · Innovation · Documentation gaps |
| **Volunteer Network** | Participation · Availability · Recognition · Leadership readiness · Wellness |
| **Governance Network** | Active proposals · Decision queues · Appeals · Policy reviews · Constitutional compliance |
| **Operations** | Workflow health · Automation health · Calendar coordination · Communication effectiveness · Platform readiness |
| **Strategic Intelligence** | Forecasts · Emerging risks · Opportunities · AI recommendations · Digital Twin summaries · Executive briefings |

**[PBA-OPI21c]** The Executive Operations Center becomes the place where statewide leaders, county coordinators, institutional partners, and executive teams understand the **health and direction of the entire ecosystem**.

**[PBA-OPI21d]** Most importantly, it answers one continuous question:

> **"If leadership could focus on only five things today, what should they be, and why?"**

**[PBA-OPI21e]** This recommendation is fully grounded in evidence collected across the platform and explained through the Community Event Ledger, Community Knowledge Graph, Digital Twins, and Knowledge Observatory.

**[PBA-OPI21f]** With Volume 3.13 complete, the Community Operating System now has a complete operational architecture spanning rules, workflows, identity, communities, missions, volunteers, leadership, intelligence, automation, attention, governance, knowledge growth, and **executive operations**.

## Burt Implementation Guidance

**[PBA-OPI22]** Implementation should:

1. Build Operational Intelligence as a **synthesis layer**
2. Integrate **Community Intelligence, Community Health Observatory, Knowledge Observatory, and Digital Twins**
3. Keep recommendations **advisory**
4. Preserve **evidence for every conclusion**
5. Generate **explainable executive briefings**
6. **Avoid duplicating** domain-specific intelligence [PBA-009]
7. Consult **Executive Operations Center** spec before executive-facing features

**[PBA-OPI22a]** Logical home: Platform Behavior schema — OperationalInsight · ReadinessAssessment · OperationalRisk · ExecutiveBriefing · ExecutiveOperationsCenter.
