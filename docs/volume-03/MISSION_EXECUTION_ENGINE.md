## Purpose

**[PBA-MEX01]** The Mission Execution Engine defines how **ideas become action** and how action becomes **measurable community impact**.

**[PBA-MEX01a]** A mission is the **primary unit of organized work** within the Community Operating System.

**[PBA-MEX01b]** Every volunteer opportunity, campaign, service project, advocacy effort, educational initiative, research project, or community improvement effort ultimately becomes a **mission**.

**[PBA-MEX01c]** The Mission Execution Engine ensures that every mission creates not only outcomes, but also **knowledge that strengthens future communities**.

---

## Guiding Principle

**[PBA-MEX02]**

> **Every mission should leave the community stronger than it found it.**

**[PBA-MEX02a]** Success is measured not only by completing work, but by **building people, knowledge, and capacity**.

## Philosophy

**[PBA-MEX03]** Traditional project management asks: Was it completed? Was it on time? Was it on budget?

**[PBA-MEX03a]** The Community Operating System also asks:

- Did people grow?
- Was knowledge captured?
- Did leadership develop?
- Can another community repeat this?
- What relationships were formed?
- What lasting impact was created?

**[PBA-MEX03b]** **Mission execution becomes community development.**

## Lifecycle Stages

**[PBA-MEX06]** Ten lifecycle stages:

### Stage 1 — Vision

**[PBA-MEX06a]** A need or opportunity is identified.

Sources: Community request · Volunteer idea · Leadership initiative · Institution request · County strategy · AI recommendation · Historical lesson

**The vision explains why the mission matters.**

### Stage 2 — Proposal

**[PBA-MEX06b]** Mission proposal defines:

Purpose · Expected outcomes · Target community · Geographic scope · Estimated effort · Leadership · Required resources · Success indicators

**Proposal becomes a governed object** [PBA-WFL06 · Mission Workflows].

### Stage 3 — Planning

**[PBA-MEX06c]** Planning establishes:

Timeline · Calendar · Volunteer needs · Budget (if applicable) · Facilities · Transportation · Risk assessment · Knowledge references · Related playbooks

**Planning should reuse existing knowledge whenever possible.**

### Stage 4 — Approval

**[PBA-MEX06d]** Approval evaluates:

Business rules · Governance · Leadership readiness · Community capacity · Mission dependencies · Scheduling conflicts

**Approval remains transparent** [PBA-002 · PBA-003].

### Stage 5 — Preparation

**[PBA-MEX06e]** Preparation includes:

Volunteer assignments · Training · Materials · Communications · Calendar confirmation · Equipment · Partnership coordination

**The mission begins before the event starts.**

### Stage 6 — Execution

**[PBA-MEX06f]** Execution tracks:

Progress · Attendance · Volunteer activity · Milestones · Issues · Adjustments · Media · Stories

**Events are recorded in real time where appropriate** [DAB-007].

### Stage 7 — Reflection

**[PBA-MEX06g]** Reflection captures:

What worked? · What failed? · Unexpected outcomes · Volunteer feedback · Community feedback · Leadership observations

**Reflection is mandatory for meaningful missions.**

### Stage 8 — Knowledge Capture

**[PBA-MEX06h]** Knowledge becomes:

Lessons · Playbook updates · Stories · Mission summaries · Community Brain entries · Training material

**Every mission contributes to organizational intelligence** [DAB-013].

### Stage 9 — Community Impact

**[PBA-MEX06i]** Impact includes:

People served · Volunteer growth · Leadership development · Knowledge generated · Relationships formed · Partnerships strengthened · Community improvements

**Impact extends beyond completion.**

### Stage 10 — Archive

**[PBA-MEX06j]** Archive preserves:

Mission history · Timeline · Media · Lessons · Playbooks · Volunteer participation · Historical significance

**Missions become reusable institutional memory.**

## Major Architectural Recommendation: Mission Operations Center

**[PBA-MEX17]** Introduce a **Mission Operations Center (MOC)** as the operational command environment for every active mission.

**[PBA-MEX17a]** Unlike a traditional project dashboard, the Mission Operations Center **continuously integrates information from across the platform** into one real-time operational view.

**[PBA-MEX17b]** The MOC combines seven operational domains:

| Domain | Combines |
|--------|----------|
| **Planning** | Mission objectives · Timeline · Dependencies · Risk register |
| **People** | Volunteers · Leadership · Mentors · Assignments · Availability |
| **Operations** | Tasks · Milestones · Calendar · Equipment · Facilities · Transportation |
| **Knowledge** | Related playbooks · Lessons learned · Community Brain references · Stories · Historical missions |
| **Intelligence** | Mission Digital Twin · Community Health signals · Volunteer workload · AI recommendations · Scheduling insights |
| **Evidence** | Photos · Documents · Meeting notes · Media · Community Event Ledger · Evidence Vault references |
| **Community Impact** | People served · Volunteer hours · Partnerships formed · Knowledge created · Leadership developed |

**[PBA-MEX17c]** The Mission Operations Center becomes the **daily workspace for mission leaders**.

**[PBA-MEX17d]** Instead of switching between calendars, task lists, documents, communications, dashboards, and reports, organizers see **one unified operational environment** that reflects the mission's current state and historical context.

**[PBA-MEX17e]** If the **Personal Operating System** supports individuals and the **Community Operating Manual** supports organizations, then the **Mission Operations Center** becomes the **execution environment** where communities transform plans into lasting impact while continuously generating knowledge for the entire network.

**[PBA-MEX17f]** Live spec: `data/registry/mission-execution-engine.json` · `missionOperationsCenter`

## Burt Implementation Guidance

**[PBA-MEX18]** Implementation should:

1. Treat missions as **long-lived operational entities**
2. **Separate mission lifecycle from task management**
3. Require **structured reflection and knowledge capture**
4. Integrate with **workflows, calendars, events, and Digital Twins**
5. **Project mission relationships** into the Community Knowledge Graph
6. Preserve **complete historical records**
7. Consult **Mission Operations Center** spec before mission-facing execution features

**[PBA-MEX18a]** Logical home: Platform Behavior schema — MissionLifecycle · MissionHealth · MissionTemplate · MissionOperationsCenter · MissionDigitalTwinProjection · MissionArchive.
