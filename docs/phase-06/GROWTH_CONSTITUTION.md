## GCN-M01 — Purpose

**[GCN-M01]** The **Growth Constitution** establishes the principles that govern how the Arkansas Student & Youth Organizing Network **expands over time**.

**[GCN-M01a]** Growth is **not pursued for its own sake**. Growth exists to strengthen **relationships, communities, leadership, and opportunities for service**.

**[GCN-M01b]** The platform should grow in ways that preserve **trust, authenticity, and local ownership** [CCN-M001 local autonomy, IOS-M13 empower locally].

**[GCN-M01c]** This is the **constitutional document for every future expansion** of the platform — new campuses, counties, schools, organizations, and states inherit GCN-001.

**[GCN-M01d]** GCN determines **how the network grows** — one of the most important architectural decisions in the entire platform.

## GCN-M03 — Philosophy

**[GCN-M03]** Growth should answer:

| Question | Owner |
|----------|-------|
| Why are we inviting people? | Personal + community intent [ICS-001 6.7] |
| How will new participants belong? | Welcome & Belonging System [WBS-001 6.5] |
| Who will welcome them? | Leadership Development + community [CLD-001 6.4] |
| What opportunities will they discover? | Opportunity Exchange [OEX-001] |
| How will the community become stronger because they joined? | Action + Learning [AOS-001, LIS-001] |

**[GCN-M03a]** **Growth without belonging is incomplete** [PEL-001, OBE-001].

**[GCN-M03b]** The platform isn't trying to maximize registrations. It's trying to create an environment where people **build relationships, contribute meaningfully, and eventually help others do the same**.

## GCN-M05 — Growth Principles

**[GCN-M05]** The platform is guided by these **permanent principles**:

### Relationship First [GCN-M05a]

Participants invite people they **know** whenever possible [RGE-M03b trust, friendship]. Trust grows naturally through relationships. The platform **supports relationship-building** rather than mass recruitment [OM-L2 relational organizing].

### Community Before Scale [GCN-M05b]

Communities should be **capable of welcoming** new participants before rapidly expanding [GCN-M10]. Healthy communities grow **sustainably** [CGS-001 renewal not failure].

### Local Leadership [GCN-M05c]

Communities remain **locally led** [CCN-M001]. Growth should **develop new leaders** rather than concentrating responsibility in a few individuals [PGL-001, CGS-M07 succession].

### Shared Opportunity [GCN-M05d]

Every new participant should quickly discover **meaningful ways to contribute** [OBE-001]. Joining should lead naturally to **participation** [JRN-001 journey stages].

### Transparency [GCN-M05e]

Participants should understand: why they were invited · how their information is used · how communities operate · how decisions are made [TPS-001, CDS-001]. Transparency strengthens trust.

### Voluntary Participation [GCN-M05f]

Participation remains **voluntary** [CP-004]. The platform should **encourage rather than pressure** [VDS-M04 invitation not assignment]. Every invitation is an **opportunity — not an obligation** [RGE-M06].

### Diversity of Communities [GCN-M05g]

The network becomes stronger by including: universities · community colleges · trade schools · future high schools · county communities · young adults not enrolled in school · future community organizations [CCN-M004 equal standing]. Different perspectives strengthen the statewide network.

### Long-Term Thinking [GCN-M05h]

Growth should prioritize: leadership development · knowledge preservation · community sustainability · institutional memory · multi-generational participation [CLS-001, LCN-001 6.11]. The objective is **lasting infrastructure** — not viral spikes.

Contribution
        ↓

**[GCN-M06a]** Growth becomes a **continuous cycle** — parallel to [Participant Journey JRN-001] and [Civic Operating Loop ACN-M04] return path to New People.

## GCN-M08 — Growth Ethics

**[GCN-M08]** The platform **avoids**:

- Manipulative engagement · artificial urgency · spam invitations
- Misleading messaging · attention-harvesting techniques · dark patterns [CAM-001 earn not abuse]

**[GCN-M08a]** **Trust is more valuable than rapid growth** [TPS-001, SEC-001].

**[GCN-M08b]** Growth mechanics must pass [PEL-M13 evaluation question] and [Growth Ethics review in welcome design WBS-001].

## GCN-M09 — Shared Responsibility

**[GCN-M09]** Every participant contributes to growth. Examples:

- Welcoming newcomers · answering questions · inviting friends
- Sharing knowledge · supporting communities · mentoring others

**[GCN-M09a]** Growth **belongs to everyone** — not a marketing department, not a central recruitment team [RGE-M01c every participant is a growth node].

## GCN-M10 — Community Readiness

**[GCN-M10]** Before significant growth, communities should ask:

- Do we have welcoming volunteers?
- Do we have meaningful opportunities?
- Do we have mentors?
- Do we have active missions?
- Do we have updated knowledge?

**[GCN-M10a]** Growth should **strengthen communities — not overwhelm them** [CGS-M05 Health Check, AOS-M09 Movement Readiness].

**[GCN-M10b]** `assessCommunityGrowthReadiness(communityId)` returns readiness signals before expansion campaigns [GCN-BG].

## GCN-M11 — Growth Reflection

**[GCN-M11]** Communities periodically reflect:

- Who joined? · Who stayed? · Who became leaders?
- What helped participants belong? · What barriers remain?

**[GCN-M11a]** Reflection guides **future growth** [LIS-001 learning, CGS-M11 renewal cycles] — not shame for slow growth.

## GCN-M12 — Invitation Impact Visibility [CP-016]

**[GCN-M12]** **Constitutional principle for the entire project:**

> **Every participant should be able to see the direct impact of the people they invited into the network.**

**[GCN-M12a]** **Not as a competition. As a story.**

```text
You invited Alex.
Alex invited Jordan.
Jordan started a volunteer team.
That team organized a food drive.
The food drive became an annual campus tradition.
Three years later it has served 1,200 families.
```

**[GCN-M12b]** Tells a participant: **Because you invited one person, this happened.**

**[GCN-M12c]** Reinforces relational organizing · celebrates long-term impact · shows small actions ripple outward — **without turning participation into a contest** [CRA-001 not gamification].

**[GCN-M12d]** Implemented in [Personal Organizing Network PON-001 6.2](PERSONAL_ORGANIZING_NETWORK.md) · [Invitation & Connection System ICS-001 6.7](INVITATION_CONNECTION_SYSTEM.md) · feeds **Living Network Graph** [GOS-M16].

## GCN-M16 — Belonging Index

**[GCN-M16]** The **Belonging Index (BI)** may be one of the most important measurements in the entire platform.

**[GCN-M16a]** Instead of asking only *"How many people joined?"* the platform also asks:

> **"How many people found a place where they felt they belonged?"**

**[GCN-M16b]** The Belonging Index is **not a score assigned to individuals**. It helps **communities reflect** on whether they are creating welcoming environments [CIS-001 coach not rank].

**[GCN-M16c]** Indicators may include:

| Indicator | Signal |
|-----------|--------|
| Joined a team | First structural belonging |
| Attended a second event | Return behavior |
| Formed new connections | Relationship Graph growth |
| Found a mentor | PGL-001 pathway |
| Became a volunteer | VDS-001 participation |
| Eventually invited someone else | Growth loop closed |
| Returned after first month | Retention over registration |

**[GCN-M16d]** Helps answer: **Are we simply growing, or are we building a community people want to remain part of?**

**[GCN-M16e]** Route: `/community/[slug]/belonging` · orchestrator: `getBelongingIndex(communityId, period?)`.

**[GCN-M16f]** Complements [Community Health CIS-001] · [Belonging Index is community reflection, not individual ranking].

**[GCN-M16g]** Feeds [Community Growth Intelligence CGIS-001 6.6] and [Growth Reflection GCN-M11] — advisory only.
