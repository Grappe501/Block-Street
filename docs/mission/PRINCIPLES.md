# Design Principles

## Core Principles

### 1. Student-Led
Leadership council, campus reps, and committee chairs are students. The platform serves them — it does not replace them.

### 2. Campus-Rooted
Every school gets its own hub. Philander Smith and UCA are peers with equal standing, not sub-brands of a statewide org.

### 3. County-Connected
Young adults not in school organize through 75 county catch-all hubs. No one is excluded because they are not enrolled.

### 4. Statewide-Connected
Local organizing links to global committees. A voter education committee at Ouachita connects with outreach at UAPB without losing campus identity.

### 5. Nonpartisan by Design
No endorsed candidates, no party branding, no issue positions baked into the product. Students decide content; the platform hosts it.

### 6. Relational, Not Broadcast
The platform grows one trusted relationship at a time. Every participant has a share link and QR code. The fundamental object is the **person**, not the campus or county.

### 7. Leadership Through Mentorship
> Leadership isn't measured by how many people you collect. It's measured by how many people you help become leaders themselves.

Network boards highlight:
- People welcomed into the network
- Volunteer hours generated
- Events attended
- Committees formed
- Civic projects completed
- New leaders developed

## Platform Guardrails

| Allowed | Not Allowed |
|---------|-------------|
| Student-created events & committees | Platform-endorsed candidates |
| Issue discussions chosen by students | Party branding or logos |
| Campus & county self-expression | Preset political direction |
| Referral / network growth tracking | Gamified recruit-count leaderboards |
| Export & outreach tools | Admin-pushed political messaging |

## Build Philosophy

- **Start minimal** — v0 does five things well
- **Version exponentially** — each release adds a complete layer
- **Document everything** — markdown + admin dashboard stay in sync
- **H: drive only** — no writes to C: drive during development
- **Commit every step** — GitHub is the source of truth; Netlify is the live mirror
