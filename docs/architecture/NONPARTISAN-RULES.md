# Nonpartisan Platform Rules

## Platform Neutrality

Block Street is infrastructure for student and youth organizing. It is **not** a campaign, PAC, party apparatus, or advocacy organization with a preset agenda.

## What the Platform Provides

- Organizing tools (signup, networks, committees, events)
- Communication channels (messaging, email export)
- Data dashboards (campus, county, state)
- Neutral issue survey tools (students define questions)

## What the Platform Never Does

| Prohibited | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| Endorse candidates | Students decide |
| Display party logos/branding | Maintains neutrality |
| Push preset issue positions | Students define priorities |
| Rank users by recruit count alone | Prevents gamification |
| Allow admin political messaging | Admins manage platform, not politics |
| Filter content by political leaning | Open student expression within guidelines |

## Content Guidelines (Future Moderation)

Student-generated content (events, committee descriptions, discussions) must:

- Not contain hate speech or harassment
- Not promote violence
- Be attributable to a student organizer
- Be reportable by other participants

Political expression **by students** is allowed. Platform **direction** is not.

## Leadership Council Role

The five founding campuses:

- Guide platform development priorities
- Represent diverse Arkansas campuses
- Do **not** control what other campuses organize around
- Do **not** speak for all Arkansas students without process

## Election Season

As election season approaches:

- Platform may add voter registration resources (neutral)
- Platform may add candidate information tools (student-curated)
- Platform may facilitate student votes/surveys on endorsements
- Platform **never** pre-selects outcomes

Students determine who and what they support. The platform helps them organize that decision collectively.
