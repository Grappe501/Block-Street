# Organizing Philosophy

## The Fundamental Object Is the Person

The platform is not a website. It is a **relationship network**.

Every participant — whether a UCA sophomore or a Pulaski County young adult — becomes a community organizer the moment they join.

## Core Question Shift

| Old Metric | New Metric |
|------------|------------|
| How many users do we have? | How many relationships are we building? |
| How many signups per campus? | How many active organizers per campus? |
| Who has the most recruits? | Who has helped the most people become leaders? |

## Every User Gets an Organizing Hub

Immediately after registration:

```
My Network
├── My Invite Link     → arkansasstudents.org/s/SteveG
├── My QR Code         → scannable recruitment
├── People I've Invited
├── Pending Invites
├── Network Growth
├── Volunteer Activity  (v1.5+)
├── Events              (v1.2+)
├── Messages            (v1.3+)
├── Teams / Committees  (v1.1+)
└── Goals               (future)
```

Think of it as a lightweight CRM for every participant.

## Example Growth Pattern

```
Steve joins → gets /s/SteveG
Steve shares QR → 10 friends join
Each friend gets THEIR OWN page:
  /s/SarahM, /s/MichaelR, /s/EmilyK
Those people invite others → graph expands naturally
```

## Campus & County Aggregation

Individual networks roll up to organizing homes:

**Campus dashboard (UCA example):**
- Total members
- Growth this week
- Top recruiters (by impact, not just count)
- Upcoming events
- Active committees

**County dashboard (Faulkner example):**
- Same structure, county-scoped

**State dashboard:**
- All numbers built from local activity

## Impact Metrics (Not Just Recruits)

Each Network Board highlights:

- People welcomed into the network
- Volunteer hours generated by their network
- Events attended by their network
- Committees their network has formed
- Civic projects completed
- Conversations started
- New leaders developed

## Why This Matters for Elections

Students determine who and what they support. Relational organizing means:

1. Trust travels through personal networks
2. Conversations happen peer-to-peer
3. Campus and county voices emerge organically
4. Statewide coordination happens through committees, not top-down messaging
5. The platform stays nonpartisan because it never picks sides — people do
