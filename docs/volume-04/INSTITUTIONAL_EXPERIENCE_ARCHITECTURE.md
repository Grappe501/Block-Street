## Purpose

**[UXB-INS01]** The Institutional Experience Architecture defines how the Community Operating System **adapts to different organizations** while preserving one unified platform.

**[UXB-INS01a]** The platform should never feel like generic software:

A school should feel like it was built for schools · A campaign should feel like it was built for campaigns · A nonprofit should feel like it was built for nonprofits · A county government should feel like it was built for counties

**[UXB-INS01b]** Yet underneath, **every institution operates on the same constitutional foundation, data model, workflow engine, and operational architecture.**

---

## Guiding Principle

**[UXB-INS02]**

> **One platform. Infinite institutional experiences.**

**[UXB-INS02a]** Organizations should **recognize themselves in the software** without fragmenting the platform.

---

## Philosophy

**[UXB-INS03]** Traditional SaaS creates separate products: CRM · Volunteer System · Learning Platform · Campaign Software · Knowledge Base · Community Platform

**[UXB-INS03a]** The Community Operating System creates **one operating system with different institutional perspectives**.

**[UXB-INS03b]** **The institution changes. The constitutional architecture does not** [CAB-001].

## Major Architectural Recommendation: Institution Factory

**[UXB-INS19]** Create an **Institution Factory (IF)** as one of the platform's most powerful architectural capabilities.

**[UXB-INS19a]** Instead of manually configuring every new organization, the Institution Factory **provisions a fully operational institution from a constitutional template**.

### Institution Factory Pipeline

```text
Institution Request
        ↓
Institution Template
        ↓
Constitution
        ↓
Operating Manual
        ↓
Workspace Configuration
        ↓
Digital Twin
        ↓
Knowledge Initialization
        ↓
AI Initialization
        ↓
Institution Launch
```

**[UXB-INS19b]** **An institution becomes operational in minutes rather than months.**

### Institution Templates

**[UXB-INS19c]** Fourteen template types, each including:

Recommended workspaces · Governance defaults · Operating Manual structure · Knowledge categories · AI profiles · Dashboard layouts · Reporting models · Lifecycle guidance

**Templates:** Community Organization · Campaign · School · Civic Academy · County Government · City Government · Church · Coalition · Nonprofit · Foundation · Advocacy Group · Professional Association · Research Center · Enterprise

### Modular Capability Packs

**[UXB-INS19d]** Institutions activate additional capabilities **without changing the platform**:

| Pack | Capabilities |
|------|-------------|
| **Civic Engagement** | Petitions · Voter registration · Public meetings · Civic education |
| **Education** | Courses · Certifications · Learning paths · Assessments |
| **Campaign** | Field operations · Events · Volunteer recruitment · Communications |
| **Disaster Response** | Incident management · Resource tracking · Emergency volunteers · Situation reports |
| **Grant Management** | Funding pipeline · Reporting · Deliverables · Compliance |
| **Research** | Publications · Citations · Research collaboration · Peer review |

**[UXB-INS19e]** **Packs extend behavior without creating separate products.**

### Cross-Institution Intelligence

**[UXB-INS19f]** Because every institution shares the same architecture, the platform can responsibly answer:

- Which counties have the strongest volunteer retention?
- Which schools are producing the most community leaders?
- Which nonprofits have reusable volunteer onboarding practices?
- Which campaigns created the most effective field training?
- Which communities have solved similar challenges?

**Knowledge becomes portable while respecting governance and permissions.**

### Institutional Ecosystem

**[UXB-INS19g]** The COS creates an ecosystem where:

Communities strengthen counties · Counties strengthen regions · Schools strengthen communities · Campaigns strengthen civic engagement · Faith organizations strengthen volunteerism · Nonprofits strengthen partnerships · Civic Academies strengthen leadership

**Every institution contributes to a larger civic network.**

**[UXB-INS19h]** Strategic vision:

> **The Community Operating System is not software for one type of organization. It is constitutional infrastructure for communities, institutions, and civic ecosystems.**

**[UXB-INS19i]** Every new organization inherits from day one:

Constitution · Operational engines · Data architecture · Experience architecture · AI Federation · Knowledge Observatory · Community Health Observatory · Executive Operations Center

**[UXB-INS19j]** **Shared civic operating infrastructure** capable of supporting thousands of organizations without fragmenting into disconnected products.

## Burt Implementation Guidance

**[UXB-INS20]** Implementation should:

1. Treat institutions as **configurations rather than separate products**
2. **Reuse operational engines** across all institution types
3. Build **Institution Operating Manuals** automatically
4. Integrate **AI, Operational Intelligence, Community Health, Digital Twins, Community Event Ledger, and Knowledge Growth**
5. Allow **branding without architectural divergence**
6. **Preserve interoperability** among institutions
7. Consult **Institution Factory** spec before institution-facing features

**[UXB-INS20a]** Logical home: Experience Architecture schema — InstitutionType · InstitutionTemplate · CapabilityPack · InstitutionFactory · InstitutionTwin.
