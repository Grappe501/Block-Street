## Purpose

**[UXB-COL01]** The Collaboration Architecture defines how people **work together** inside the Community Operating System.

**[UXB-COL01a]** Collaboration is **not** a chat feature. It is the **connective tissue** between communities, missions, leadership, knowledge, governance, and relationships.

**[UXB-COL01b]** The platform should become the place where communities **think together, plan together, solve problems together, and preserve what they learned together.**

---

## Guiding Principle

**[UXB-COL02]**

> **Collaboration should strengthen relationships, not merely exchange messages.**

**[UXB-COL02a]** Every interaction should **deepen trust and collective understanding.**

---

## Philosophy

**[UXB-COL03]** Traditional collaboration tools revolve around **conversations**.

**[UXB-COL03a]** The Community Operating System revolves around **shared work**.

**[UXB-COL03b]** Conversations should always remain connected to:

Communities · Missions · Knowledge · Decisions · People · History

**[UXB-COL03c]** **Discussion without context becomes difficult to reuse.**

---

## Collaboration Architecture

**[UXB-COL04]** Every collaborative interaction follows the same lifecycle:

```text
Purpose
      ↓
People
      ↓
Conversation
      ↓
Decision
      ↓
Action
      ↓
Knowledge
      ↓
History
```

**[UXB-COL04a]** **Every collaboration leaves behind institutional memory** [DAB-007].

---

## Collaboration Principles

**[UXB-COL05]** Every collaborative experience should:

Strengthen relationships · Reduce duplication · Capture knowledge · Support transparency · Respect participant attention · Encourage learning · Remain discoverable · Preserve context

## Major Architectural Recommendation: Collaborative Intelligence Network

**[UXB-COL24]** Create a **Collaborative Intelligence Network (CIN)** as the layer that transforms conversations into **lasting organizational intelligence**.

**[UXB-COL24a]** Unlike traditional chat systems where information disappears into message history, the CIN continuously understands the **operational significance** of collaboration.

**[UXB-COL24b]** The CIN organizes collaboration into six connected layers:

### Layer 1 — Conversation

Discussions · Questions · Comments · Voice notes · Meeting chat

### Layer 2 — Collaboration

Shared notes · Documents · Whiteboards · Planning · Brainstorming

### Layer 3 — Decisions

Proposals · Reviews · Approvals · Governance · Action items

### Layer 4 — Knowledge

Lessons · Stories · Playbooks · Community Brain · Mission reflections

### Layer 5 — Relationships

Mentorship · Teams · Communities · Partnerships · Leadership

### Layer 6 — Intelligence

AI summaries · Community Intelligence · Operational Intelligence · Knowledge recommendations · Relationship insights

**[UXB-COL24c]** The CIN continuously asks:

- Did this conversation produce a decision?
- Did this meeting generate a lesson?
- Should this discussion become a playbook?
- Who else could benefit from this knowledge?
- Are two communities solving the same problem independently?
- Should a mentor be introduced?

**[UXB-COL24d]** Instead of becoming an archive of forgotten messages, collaboration becomes a **continuously expanding source of institutional knowledge**.

**[UXB-COL24e]** Architectural theme:

> **Conversations are valuable, but the knowledge and relationships they create are even more valuable.**

**[UXB-COL24f]** The COS preserves not only what people said, but **what they learned together, what they decided together, and how those experiences strengthen every future community.**

## Burt Implementation Guidance

**[UXB-COL25]** Implementation should:

1. **Connect every conversation** to operational context
2. **Preserve collaboration history** permanently
3. Integrate collaboration with **Community Event Ledger, Knowledge Graph, Digital Twins, and AI**
4. Design collaboration around **shared work** rather than chat
5. Build **asynchronous collaboration** as a first-class capability
6. Make collaboration **searchable and reusable**
7. Consult **Collaborative Intelligence Network** spec before collaboration-facing features

**[UXB-COL25a]** Logical home: Experience Architecture schema — CollaborationDomain · CollaborationSpace · DecisionThread · CollaborativeIntelligenceNetwork.
