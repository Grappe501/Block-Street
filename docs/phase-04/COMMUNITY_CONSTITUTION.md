> **Communities are built by people, strengthened by relationships, and sustained by shared purpose.**

This is where the software stops being a platform and becomes **movement infrastructure**. Everything before this defined Arkansas, people, and relationships. Now we define **community** — one of the most important philosophical documents in the project, because **every future feature will belong to some community**.

## CCN-M01 — Purpose

**[CCN-M01]** This document establishes the **constitutional framework shared by every community** within the Arkansas Student & Youth Organizing Network.

**[CCN-M01a]** Every county, campus, committee, project, and future organizing space **inherits these principles** while developing its own unique identity.

**[CCN-M01b]** The platform provides the **framework**. The members create the **culture** [CP-004].

**[CCN-M01c]** Terminology: **Community Constitution** (inherited statewide) vs **Community Charter** (written per community) [CCN-M17].

**[CCN-M01d]** Phase 4 answers: **Where do we work together?** — from individuals to **living institutions**.

---

## CCN-M02 — Guiding Principle

**[CCN-M02]**

> **Communities are built by people, strengthened by relationships, and sustained by shared purpose.**

**[CCN-M02a]** The software exists to **support communities — not define them** [OM-L1, CP-004].

**[CCN-M02b]** Complementary principle:

> **The platform provides the structure. The people create the culture.**

**[CCN-M02c]** Evaluation question [PEL-M13]: *Does this strengthen relationships, deepen belonging, and help people grow into community builders?*

## CCN-M04 — Definition of a Community

**[CCN-M04]** A community is a group of people connected by a **shared place, purpose, interest, or commitment**.

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| County | Pulaski, Ashley |
| University | UCA, University of Arkansas |
| Community college | Future expansion |
| Trade school | Future expansion |
| High school | Future [ADT-M15] |
| Committee | Campus Outreach, Communications |
| Volunteer team | Food drive crew |
| Project | Campus Cleanup |
| Alumni network | Future [JRN alumni] |
| Regional collaboration | Central Arkansas coalition |
| Statewide initiative | Future |

**[CCN-M04a]** All communities inherit the **same constitutional philosophy** [CCN-M01].

**[CCN-M04b]** A community is a **first-class graph entity** [REL-M01] running a Community OS [CCN-M18].

---

## CCN-M05 — Equal Standing

**[CCN-M05]** **No community is inherently more important than another** [OM-006].

**[CCN-M05a]** A small rural county deserves the **same architectural support** as the state's largest university.

**[CCN-M05b]** Every organizing space receives the **same foundational capabilities** — communities differ in **participation**, not in **importance**.

**[CCN-M05c]** Discovery may highlight **needs** [OIS-M09] — never **rank** [OIS-M14, CRA-M03].

## CCN-M09 — Community Identity

**[CCN-M09]** Every community develops its own identity over time:

Mission · Vision · Goals · Traditions · History · Culture · Current priorities · Community stories · Local resources

**[CCN-M09a]** Identity grows **naturally** — seeded by [Community DNA CID-M13], enriched by participation [CID-M10].

**[CCN-M09b]** Identity layer sits **above** inherited Constitution, **below** daily Activity [CCN-M03].

**[CCN-M09c]** UCA feels different from UA Fayetteville because **Charter and identity differ** — not because software differs.

## CCN-M10 — Community Leadership

**[CCN-M10]** Leadership exists to **serve the community** — not to rank above it.

| Leadership responsibility |
|---------------------------|
| Facilitating participation |
| Developing others |
| Coordinating projects |
| Welcoming new members |
| Protecting community culture |
| Sharing knowledge |

**[CCN-M10a]** Leadership should **rotate and expand** as new participants grow [PGL-001, JRN-M06].

**[CCN-M10b]** No permanent platform-imposed leaders — communities choose their own [CP-004].

## CCN-M11 — Community Membership

**[CCN-M11]** Membership is based on **participation rather than status**.

**[CCN-M11a]** Participants may belong to **multiple communities simultaneously** [PEP-M08]:

- Their county · Their university · A statewide committee · A volunteer project · An alumni network

**[CCN-M11b]** Communities should **complement rather than compete** with one another.

**[CCN-M11c]** Graph edges — not duplicate records [REL-M01, PDT-M13].

---

## CCN-M12 — Community Relationships

**[CCN-M12]** Communities are **connected** — no community exists in isolation:

| Relationship | Example |
|--------------|---------|
| Campus ↔ County | Student serves home county |
| County ↔ County | Neighboring collaboration |
| Campus ↔ Campus | Cross-institution project |
| Committee ↔ Project | Outreach committee runs food drive |
| Project ↔ Volunteer team | Temporary team edges |
| Regional ↔ Statewide | Coalition to initiative |

**[CCN-M12a]** [Statewide Collaboration Network](STATEWIDE_COLLABORATION_NETWORK.md) [SCN-001] specifies statewide collaboration infrastructure.

**[CCN-M12b]** Relationship graph queryable statewide [REL-M01].

## CCN-M13 — Community Legacy

**[CCN-M13]** Every community **preserves its history** — institutional memory strengthens future generations:

Projects · Milestones · Events · Volunteer stories · Leadership transitions · Photos · Resources · Meeting notes

**[CCN-M13a]** [Community Legacy System](COMMUNITY_LEGACY_SYSTEM.md) [CLS-001] implements at community level — parallel to participant [Civic Journey Timeline CJT-001]. Legacy carries the past **into the future**.

**[CCN-M13b]** Append-only archive pattern [KDG-M07, STS-M16] — legacy layer [CCN-M03].

## CCN-M17 — Community Charters Architecture

**[CCN-M17]** **Signature architecture:** Every community eventually has a **Community Charter** built on this Constitution.

| Layer | Scope | Who writes |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **Platform Constitution** | Entire ASYON | Founding principles |
| **Community Constitution** | All communities | This document — inherited |
| **Community Charter** | One community | That community's members |

**[CCN-M17a]** Charter may include:

| Charter element |
|-----------------|
| Our mission this year |
| Our goals for this semester |
| Our meeting rhythm |
| Our traditions |
| Our values |
| Our current projects |
| How new members get involved |
| Annual priorities |
| Success stories |
| Community agreements |

**[CCN-M17b]** Charters become **living documents** — reflect evolution over time [KDG-M07 audit trail].

**[CCN-M17c]** Charter surfaces on **Community HQ** [4.3] — informs [Opportunity Exchange OEX-001].

**[CCN-M17d]** Orchestrator: `getCommunityCharter(communityId)` — extends DNA [CID-M13].

**[CCN-M17e]** V1: mission + welcome + priorities stub; full Charter editor v1.1.

## CCN-BG — Burt Implementation Guidance

**[CCN-BG]** Implementation should:

1. **Treat every community as first-class entity** — graph node + OS instance [CCN-M04]
2. **Provide identical foundational capabilities** — equal standing [CCN-M05]
3. **Separate identity from activity** — hierarchy layers [CCN-M03]
4. **Support future community types** without redesign [CCN-M04]
5. **Preserve history over time** — memory + continuity [CCN-M13, CCN-M16]
6. **Enable local customization** — Charters within Constitution [CCN-M17]

**[CCN-BG-a]** Recommended structure:

```
src/lib/community/CommunityConstitution.ts    — inherited principles (read-only)
src/lib/community/CommunityCharter.ts       — per-community document
src/lib/community/assembleCommunityHQ.ts
```

**[CCN-BG-b]** Gate: no community type without Constitution inheritance + Charter schema.

**[CCN-BG-c]** **Required reading** before any Phase 4+ community feature.
