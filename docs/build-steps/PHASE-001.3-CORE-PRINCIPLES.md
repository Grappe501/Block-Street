## Purpose

**[CP-PURPOSE]** This document establishes the permanent design principles of the Arkansas Student & Youth Organizing Network.

**[CP-PURPOSEa]** These principles are intended to remain stable across future versions of the platform.

**[CP-PURPOSEb]** Every feature, workflow, interface, database design, and future enhancement should align with these principles.

**[CP-PURPOSEc]** When tradeoffs arise, these principles take precedence over convenience or short-term implementation decisions.

---

## CP-001 — Every Young Arkansan Has an Organizing Home

**[CP-001]** No participant should be excluded because of where they live or whether they attend school.

**[CP-001a]** Every person should have a clear path into the platform through:

- An educational institution
- A county organizing hub
- Their own personal network

**[CP-001b]** The platform should continuously reduce barriers to participation.

*Aligns with:* [OH-001, OH-002, OH-003] · [NS-002]

---

## CP-002 — Equal Standing

**[CP-002]** Every county. Every educational institution. Every participant. Begins with equal capabilities.

**[CP-002a]** No county, campus, or institution receives permanent structural advantages within the platform.

**[CP-002b]** Leadership emerges through participation rather than predetermined status.

*Aligns with:* [ER-001] · [NS-Q5]

---

## CP-003 — Relationships Come Before Technology

**[CP-003]** The platform exists to strengthen human relationships.

**[CP-003a]** Technology is the infrastructure. People are the purpose.

**[CP-003b]** Every feature should encourage meaningful connections rather than passive consumption.

*Aligns with:* [OP-001] · [NS-Q1] · [NS-010]

---

## CP-004 — Local Communities Own Their Voice

**[CP-004]** Campus communities determine their own priorities.

**[CP-004a]** County communities determine their own priorities.

**[CP-004b]** The statewide platform provides tools and connections but does not dictate agendas or outcomes.

*Aligns with:* [NS-009] · [NS-003g]

---

## CP-005 — Nonpartisan Infrastructure

**[CP-005]** The platform does not exist to promote or oppose any political party or candidate.

**[CP-005a]** Its purpose is to provide organizing infrastructure that enables participants to engage in civic life and determine their own priorities.

*Aligns with:* [PI-002b] · [NS-003g]

---

## CP-006 — Leadership Is Earned

**[CP-006]** Leadership is demonstrated through service, initiative, collaboration, mentorship, and participation.

**[CP-006a]** The software should create opportunities for leadership to emerge rather than assigning permanent authority.

*Aligns with:* [ER-001d] · [NS-011]

---

## CP-007 — Privacy by Design

**[CP-007]** Participants should understand what information they share and how it is used.

**[CP-007a]** The platform should collect only the information necessary to provide its services.

**[CP-007b]** Privacy and user trust should remain central design considerations throughout future development.

---

## CP-008 — Mobile First

**[CP-008]** The majority of participants will interact using phones.

**[CP-008a]** Every feature should be designed for mobile use before desktop optimization.

**[CP-008b]** Desktop experiences should enhance—not define—the platform.

---

## CP-009 — Accessibility

**[CP-009]** The platform should strive to be usable by participants with diverse abilities and technical experience.

**[CP-009a]** Navigation should be simple, clear, and inclusive.

**[CP-009b]** Accessibility should be considered throughout design and implementation.

---

## CP-010 — Build for Expansion

**[CP-010]** Every system should be designed so additional schools, counties, organizations, and future capabilities can be added without redesigning the core architecture.

**[CP-010a]** The platform should anticipate growth rather than resist it.

*Aligns with:* [GS-002] · [NS-Q4] · [PI-001 note on swappable branding]

---

## CP-011 — Visible Progress Builds Momentum

**[CP-011]** Participants should be able to see the collective progress of the statewide network.

Examples include:

- Counties represented
- Educational institutions represented
- New organizers
- Active committees
- Volunteer participation
- Community projects

**[CP-011a]** Visible progress encourages participation and shared ownership.

*Aligns with:* [NS-005] · Outreach gap dashboard

---

## CP-012 — Local Identity Matters

**[CP-012]** Every county page should feel like that county.

**[CP-012a]** Every campus page should feel connected to that campus.

**[CP-012b]** Within legal and branding boundaries, the platform should reflect the identity, history, geography, and character of each community.

**[CP-012c]** Participants should feel:

> "This space was built for people like me."

*Aligns with:* [NS-007a, NS-008a] · School page personalization

---

## CP-013 — The Platform Exists to Teach Leadership

**[CP-013]** Every interaction should help participants grow.

Participants should gradually learn how to:

- Recruit
- Organize
- Communicate
- Collaborate
- Solve problems
- Build teams
- Lead projects

**[CP-013a]** The software should function as both an organizing platform and a leadership development platform.

*Aligns with:* Golden Circle teaching · [TC-*] modules (future)

---

## CP-014 — Continuous Improvement

**[CP-014]** The platform is designed to evolve.

**[CP-014a]** Every release should improve usability, capability, and participant experience while preserving the core constitutional principles.

**[CP-014b]** Implementation feedback should continuously inform future versions.

*Aligns with:* Burt build doctrine · Version roadmap

---

## CP-015 — The Network Outlasts Individuals

**[CP-015]** The platform should be designed so knowledge, relationships, and organizational capacity continue even as participants graduate, relocate, or move into new stages of life.

**[CP-015a]** Communities should become stronger over time rather than restarting with each new generation.

*Aligns with:* [PI-004] · [NS-003e] · [NS-014]

---

## CT-001 — Constitutional Test

**[CT-001]** Before approving any significant feature, ask:

| # | Question | Aligns With |
|---|----------|-------------|
| CT-Q1 | Does it strengthen **relationships**? | CP-003, NS-Q1 |
| CT-Q2 | Does it preserve **equal opportunity**? | CP-002, NS-Q5 |
| CT-Q3 | Does it **empower local communities**? | CP-004, NS-Q2 |
| CT-Q4 | Does it protect **participant trust**? | CP-007 |
| CT-Q5 | Does it help **develop leaders**? | CP-006, CP-013 |
| CT-Q6 | Can it **scale across Arkansas**? | CP-010, NS-Q4 |
| CT-Q7 | Does it align with the platform's **nonpartisan purpose**? | CP-005 |
| CT-Q8 | Does it improve the **participant experience**? | CP-008, CP-009 |
| CT-Q9 | Does it preserve **future flexibility**? | CP-010, CP-014 |
| CT-Q10 | Will this still make sense **five years from now**? | CP-015 |

**[CT-001a]** If the answer to one or more of these questions is **"no,"** the feature should be reconsidered.

### Relationship to North Star Questions [NS-013]

The Constitutional Test extends the Five North Star Questions with trust, leadership development, participant experience, flexibility, and long-term durability.

---

## Closing Statement

**[CP-CLOSE]** These principles are intended to guide the platform through future growth while preserving its core identity.

Technology will evolve. Features will change. Interfaces will improve.

**The organizing philosophy, commitment to equal opportunity, and focus on building relationships should remain constant.**

---

## Change Protocol

These principles are **immutable doctrine** — not casually editable.

| Action | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Clarify wording | User approval + document version bump |
| Add principle | User approval + new CP-ID + BUILD-LOG entry |
| Modify principle | User approval + supersede old ID + explicit rationale |
| Remove principle | Requires unanimous user decision — strongly discouraged |

Implementation details change. These principles do not.
