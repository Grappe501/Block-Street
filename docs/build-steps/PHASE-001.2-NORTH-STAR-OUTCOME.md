## NS-001 — Purpose

**[NS-001]** This document defines the ultimate destination of the Arkansas Student & Youth Organizing Network.

**[NS-001a]** It does **not** describe the first release.

**[NS-001b]** It describes the future state that every version of the platform should move toward.

**[NS-001c]** Every feature, every page, every database table, and every future enhancement should be evaluated against this North Star.

---

## NS-002 — The North Star

**[NS-002]** The North Star:

> **Every young Arkansan has an organizing home, a trusted network, and the tools to build relationships, develop leadership, strengthen their community, and participate in civic life.**

---

## NS-003 — Vision of Success

**[NS-003]** When the platform reaches maturity:

**[NS-003a]** A student attending any participating educational institution in Arkansas can immediately find their campus community.

**[NS-003b]** A young adult who is not enrolled in school can immediately find their county community.

**[NS-003c]** Every participant can invite others into their own trusted network.

**[NS-003d]** Ideas move across campuses.

**[NS-003e]** Experience moves across generations.

**[NS-003f]** Leadership grows from the local level upward.

**[NS-003g]** The platform connects people without taking ownership of their voices.

---

## NS-004 — What Success Looks Like

**[NS-004]** Success is not measured only by user registrations.

**[NS-004a]** Success is measured by the strength of the organizing network that develops.

**[NS-004b]** The platform should increase:

- Relationships
- Participation
- Leadership development
- Collaboration
- Local engagement
- Community problem-solving
- Volunteerism
- Civic knowledge
- Student-to-student mentoring
- County-to-county collaboration

---

## NS-005 — The Arkansas Map (Maturity Vision)

**[NS-005]** At maturity:

**[NS-005a]** Every county is represented.

**[NS-005b]** Every college and university is represented.

**[NS-005c]** Every community college is represented.

**[NS-005d]** Every technical school is represented.

**[NS-005e]** Every trade school is represented.

**[NS-005f]** Future participating high schools are represented.

**[NS-005g]** Every organizing community has a digital home.

---

## NS-006 — Personal Network Vision

**[NS-006]** Every participant owns a personal organizing network.

Each person receives:

- A personal invite link
- A QR code
- A network dashboard
- A relationship tree
- Growth metrics
- Volunteer activity
- Committee participation
- Event history

**[NS-006a]** The platform grows one relationship at a time.

---

## NS-007 — Campus Vision

**[NS-007]** Every educational institution becomes its own organizing community.

Each campus page should eventually provide:

- Student directory (privacy-aware)
- Campus events
- Committees
- Projects
- Volunteer opportunities
- Announcements
- Resource library
- Leadership opportunities
- Campus analytics

**[NS-007a]** Students should immediately feel: **"This is my campus."**

---

## NS-008 — County Vision

**[NS-008]** Every county becomes a permanent organizing community.

County pages should eventually include:

- Young adult organizing hub
- Local projects
- Volunteer opportunities
- Community events
- Committee spaces
- Resources
- Growth metrics

**[NS-008a]** Residents should immediately feel: **"This is my county."**

---

## NS-009 — Statewide Vision

**[NS-009]** The statewide platform exists to connect—not control.

**[NS-009a]** Its purpose is to make it easier for communities to:

- Learn from one another
- Share successful ideas
- Coordinate statewide initiatives
- Discover opportunities
- Build relationships across Arkansas

**[NS-009b]** The state level strengthens local communities while preserving their independence.

---

## NS-010 — Technology Vision

**[NS-010]** The technology should become nearly invisible.

**[NS-010a]** Participants should think about:

- People
- Communities
- Projects
- Relationships

**[NS-010b]** Not software.

**[NS-010c]** The software quietly supports organizing in the background.

---

## NS-011 — Leadership Vision

**[NS-011]** Leadership is developed through participation.

Every participant should have opportunities to:

- Organize events
- Recruit others
- Lead committees
- Mentor new members
- Build projects
- Volunteer
- Solve local problems

**[NS-011a]** Leadership is earned through contribution.

---

## NS-012 — Growth Vision

**[NS-012]** Growth should occur naturally through trusted relationships.

The platform should encourage:

```
Person → Friend
Friend → Friend
Campus → Campus
County → County
Community → Community
```

**[NS-012a]** Rather than relying primarily on advertising or centralized recruitment.

---

## NS-013 — Five North Star Questions

**[NS-013]** Before any feature is approved, ask:

### NS-Q1 — Relationships
**Does this help people build relationships?**

### NS-Q2 — Local Communities
**Does this strengthen local communities?**

### NS-Q3 — Participation
**Does this increase participation?**

### NS-Q4 — Scale
**Can this scale to every county and educational institution?**

### NS-Q5 — Equal Opportunity
**Does this preserve equal opportunity for every organizing community?**

**[NS-013a]** If the answer to any of these questions is **"no,"** the feature should be redesigned or deferred.

---

## NS-014 — End-State Statement

**[NS-014]** The completed platform should function as Arkansas's statewide youth organizing infrastructure.

Every young person should be able to:

- Find their organizing home
- Build meaningful relationships
- Recruit others
- Join local efforts
- Lead community projects
- Collaborate across Arkansas
- Leave behind a stronger network for the next generation

**[NS-014a]** The platform's lasting success will not be measured by the software itself, but by the strength, resilience, and continuity of the communities it helps people build.
