## PI-001 — Platform Working Name

**Project Name (Working Title):**  
**Arkansas Student & Youth Organizing Network (ASYON)**

> **Note:** This is a temporary working name used throughout the design process. The final public-facing name and branding will be selected later. All architecture must allow the platform name, domain, logos, colors, and branding to be changed without requiring structural changes.

---

## PI-002 — Platform Identity Statement

**[PI-002]** The Arkansas Student & Youth Organizing Network is a statewide digital organizing platform designed to connect young people across Arkansas through their schools, colleges, universities, trade schools, communities, and counties.

**[PI-002a]** The platform exists to help young Arkansans organize with one another, build relationships, develop leadership, communicate effectively, coordinate local and statewide activities, and participate more fully in civic life.

**[PI-002b]** Rather than directing students toward predetermined political outcomes, the platform provides the infrastructure that allows participants to discover issues, organize around shared interests, recruit others into their own networks, and collectively determine the priorities they wish to pursue.

**[PI-002c]** Every participant begins with the same opportunity to build relationships, contribute ideas, and strengthen their local community.

---

## MS-001 — Mission Statement

**[MS-001]** To provide every young Arkansan with an organizing home where they can build relationships, develop leadership, strengthen their community, and participate in civic life through a statewide network that connects local communities while preserving local independence.

---

## VS-001 — Vision Statement

**[VS-001]** To create the largest interconnected youth organizing network in Arkansas, where every county, every educational institution, and every young adult has an equal opportunity to participate, collaborate, and build lasting civic leadership.

---

## PU-001 — Purpose

**[PU-001]** The platform exists to solve several problems that currently limit youth participation:

- Students often organize only within their own campus.
- Young adults not enrolled in school frequently lack an organizing home.
- Colleges rarely communicate with one another.
- Local efforts often remain isolated.
- New organizers have difficulty finding others who share their interests.
- Young leaders frequently graduate without transferring knowledge to the next generation.

**[PU-001a]** The platform addresses these challenges by creating a connected statewide organizing infrastructure.

---

## AU-001 — Primary Audience

**[AU-001]** The platform serves:

- College and university students
- Community college students
- Technical and trade school students
- Young adults who are not currently enrolled in school
- Future participating high school students (planned expansion)

---

## GS-001 — Geographic Scope (Initial)

**[GS-001]** Initial scope:

- Entire State of Arkansas
- All 75 counties
- Every public and private college and university
- Community colleges
- Technical institutes
- Trade schools

---

## GS-002 — Geographic Scope (Future)

**[GS-002]** Future scope:

- High schools
- Youth organizations
- Expansion beyond Arkansas (if desired)

---

## OP-001 — Organizing Philosophy

**[OP-001]** The platform is built on one fundamental belief:

> **People organize through relationships.**

**[OP-001a]** Technology does not replace organizing. Technology strengthens organizing.

**[OP-001b]** The software exists to help people connect, recruit, collaborate, communicate, and lead.

**[OP-001c]** Relationships—not algorithms—are the foundation of the network.

---

## OH-001 — Organizing Home: Educational Institution

**[OH-001]** Students organize through the educational institution they attend.

Examples include:

- Universities
- Colleges
- Community colleges
- Technical schools
- Trade schools
- Future participating high schools

---

## OH-002 — Organizing Home: County

**[OH-002]** Young adults who are not enrolled in school organize through their county.

**[OH-002a]** Every Arkansas county receives an organizing hub.

---

## OH-003 — Organizing Home: Personal Network

**[OH-003]** Every participant automatically receives a personal organizing space.

Each user receives:

- Personal invite link
- QR code
- Network dashboard
- Relationship map
- Recruitment tools
- Growth statistics

**[OH-003a]** This personal network becomes the primary engine for relational organizing.

---

## ER-001 — Equal Representation Principle

**[ER-001]** Every organizing community begins with equal standing.

**[ER-001a]** There are no permanent founding campuses.

**[ER-001b]** There are no permanent leadership institutions.

**[ER-001c]** Every county and every educational institution receives the same organizational capabilities.

**[ER-001d]** Leadership is earned through participation rather than granted through designation.

---

## PI-003 — Platform Motto

**[PI-003]** Connect Locally. Organize Statewide. Lead Together.

---

## PI-004 — Long-Term Identity

**[PI-004]** This platform is not simply a website.

**[PI-004a]** It is intended to become Arkansas's permanent youth organizing infrastructure, providing future generations with a shared digital space where relationships, leadership, and civic participation can continue to grow regardless of changes in organizations, elections, or individual leaders.
