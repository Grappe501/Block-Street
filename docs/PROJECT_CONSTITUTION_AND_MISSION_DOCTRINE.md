## 1. Platform Identity

### Name

**Gather Arkansas**

Neutral. Nonpartisan. Action-oriented. Statewide.

### Mission (One Sentence)

> **Gather Arkansas gives every young Arkansan ages 16–24 an organizing home — through their school, their county, or their personal network — to build collective voice and voting power.**

### What We Are

A **nonpartisan youth organizing infrastructure** for Arkansas. We are building a **voting block** — a collective voice that students and young workers determine for themselves.

### What We Are Not

- Not a campaign or PAC
- Not affiliated with any political party
- Not an official arm of any school or university
- Not a top-down organization with preset leadership

### Core Audience

| Who | Age | How They Enter |
|-----|-----|----------------|
| College & university students | 16–24 | County → School |
| Working youth not in school | 16–24 | County only |
| Community college students (staged) | 16–24 | County → School |
| Trade school students (staged) | 16–24 | County → School |
| High school students (future) | 16–18 | County → School |

**Primary audience:** Working-class youth 16–24 — in school or not. We must welcome and keep space for non-students in this age bracket.

### Affiliation Model

**Honor system.** Users pick the county their school is in, then pick their school. No verification gate in V1 — trust first, verify later if needed.

## 2. North Star Outcome

> **Every young Arkansan can find an organizing home through their school, their county, or their personal network.**

By **November 3, 2026**:

- Students find each other by campus and county
- Every participant recruits through personal share links and QR codes
- The statewide map shows who's represented and who still needs outreach
- Campuses organize toward **voter registration** when school starts
- Students build **collective voice and voting block power** — they decide who and what to support
- The platform never forces a political direction

## 3. Core Principles

### 1. Youth-Led
Students run their own moderation, rules, and committees. The platform provides framework and teaching — **they** determine governance.

### 2. Nonpartisan Infrastructure
We build tools. Students build direction. No endorsed candidates. No party branding.

### 3. Equal Standing — No Privileged Campuses
**Every person, every campus, and every county begins with exactly the same capabilities.**

There is no leadership council. There are no founding campuses with permanent advantages. There is no hierarchy of schools. Differences emerge only through activity and participation.

### 4. Relational Organizing First
Every participant gets a share link, QR code, and network board. The fundamental object is the **person**, not the institution.

### 5. No School Impersonation
> "An independent student organizing network for students connected to [School Name]. Not affiliated with or endorsed by the institution."

### 6. Privacy-First
Honor-system affiliation. Minimal data. No unnecessary collection from minors.

### 7. Mobile-First
Design for phones. Most organizing happens on mobile.

### 8. Teaching Built In
**They do not know WHAT to do.** We teach WHY we exist, HOW we gain power, then WHAT to do next. See [Section 7](#7-teaching-system-golden-circle).

### 9. Distributed Leadership
Leaders emerge through recruiting, event organizing, committee service, and helping others — not through appointment or designation.

### 10. Expandable by Design
Start with colleges and universities. Add trade schools, junior colleges, and high schools as people join and onboard them.

## 4. Platform Boundaries

### The Platform MUST NOT

| Prohibition | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Endorse candidates or parties | Students decide collectively |
| Display party logos or branding | Maintains neutrality |
| Use official school logos without license | Trademark protection |
| Claim official school endorsement | Legal protection |
| Create permanent governing bodies | Equal standing |
| Rank users solely by recruit count | Prevents gamification |
| Push preset issue positions | Student ownership |
| Collect unnecessary youth data | Privacy / safety |

### The Platform MUST

| Requirement | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Show clear independence disclaimers on every campus page | Legal protection |
| Use color inspiration, not exact trademark replication | Brand safety |
| Give every hub identical starting capabilities | Equal standing |
| Surface outreach gaps (unrepresented schools/counties) | Mission visibility |
| Teach organizing before expecting action | Youth don't know yet |

## 5. Organizing Model

### Federation, Not Hierarchy

```
                    Gather Arkansas
           (connects — does not direct)
      ┌────────────┼────────────┐
  Universities   Counties   (Future: Trade/JR/HS)
      │             │             │
  Student        Youth         Student
  Networks       Networks      Networks
      │             │             │
  Personal Network Boards (every user)
```

### Three Organizing Homes

**Campus Hub** — for students connected to a school  
**County Hub** — for youth not in school + geographic home for all  
**Personal Network Board** — every user's recruitment tree, share link, QR code

### Signup Flow

**Path A — Student (honor system):**
```
Join → Pick County → Pick School → Profile → Interests → Share Link + QR → Network Board
```

**Path B — Non-student youth (16–24):**
```
Join → Pick County → Profile → Interests → Share Link + QR → Network Board
```

### Relational Organizing

Every user receives:
- Personal share URL: `gatherarkansas.org/s/[name]`
- Downloadable QR code
- Network board showing people they've invited
- Growth stats (breadth of impact, not just count)

## 7. Teaching System (Golden Circle)

> **Start with WHY. Then HOW. Then WHAT.**

Young people don't know how to organize. This platform must teach them — not just give them tools.

### WHY — The Emotional Call to Action

**This is the first thing every user experiences.**

> You are part of a generation that will decide the future of Arkansas.  
> Right now, decisions are being made about your schools, your jobs, your communities, your rights — and most young people aren't at the table.  
> Not because you don't care. Because nobody showed you how to sit down together.  
> **Gather Arkansas exists because when young Arkansans organize — when we show up as one voice — we become impossible to ignore.**  
> This isn't about a party. This isn't about someone telling you who to vote for.  
> This is about **you and your generation building the power to decide for yourselves.**

### HOW — Gaining Collective Voice & Voting Power

> Power comes from numbers. Numbers come from relationships.  
> When you invite your friends, and they invite theirs, you aren't just signing people up — you're building a **voting block**.  
> One campus talking is a whisper. Ten campuses connected is a voice. All 75 counties organized is a **force**.  
> We gain power by: showing up together, registering to vote together, deciding together, and acting together.

### WHAT — The Tools (Built Over Time)

| Tool | Version |
|------|---------|
| Find your campus / county | V1 |
| Sign up + share link + QR | V1 |
| Personal network board | V1 |
| Statewide map + outreach gaps | V1 |
| Committees | v1.1 |
| Events + voter registration push | v1.2 |
| Messaging + outreach | v1.3 |
| Surveys + collective voice votes | v1.4 |
| Roberts Rules training + governance guides | v1.1+ |
| Full organizing curriculum | Ongoing |

### Teaching Modules (Planned)

1. **Why We Gather** — emotional + psychological call to action
2. **How We Build Power** — collective voice, voting blocks, relationships
3. **How to Recruit** — share links, QR codes, relational organizing
4. **How to Form a Committee** — purpose, members, goals
5. **Roberts Rules of Order** — running meetings, making decisions
6. **How to Set Your Own Rules** — moderation, standards, campus governance
7. **How to Register Voters** — school-start push guide
8. **How to Take a Collective Position** — surveys, delegates, campus votes

## 8. Governance & Moderation

### Student-Run, Platform-Guided

**Students determine all rules for themselves.** The platform provides:

- Suggested moderation framework
- Guide to electing/recognizing a campus moderator
- Template community standards (starting point, not final)
- Roberts Rules teaching resources
- Conflict resolution suggestions

### Moderation Framework (Starting Point — Students Adapt)

Each campus/county should eventually have:
- **Campus/County Moderator** — recognized by local members, not appointed by platform
- **Community Standards** — written by local members
- **Reporting mechanism** — members flag content
- **Appeal process** — peer review

### Platform Role in Moderation

- Provide tools (report, hide, ban at campus level)
- Provide training on healthy moderation
- Enforce only platform-wide safety minimums (no hate speech, no violence, no harassment)
- **Never** moderate political content based on leaning

## 11. Legal & Branding Guardrails

### Campus Page Disclaimer (Required on Every School Page)

> An independent student organizing network for students connected to [School Name]. Not affiliated with or endorsed by [School Name] or its administration.

### Branding Rules

| Allowed | Not Allowed |
|---------|-------------|
| School name in plain text | Official logos without license |
| City and county identity | Exact trademark color reproduction |
| Color *inspiration* (e.g., "razorback red tone") | Mascot images |
| Public facts (founding year, enrollment, location) | Implying official partnership |
| Link to official school website | Using school branding in platform logo |

### Personalized Campus Pages Should Include

- School name, city, county
- Type (public/private/HBCU)
- Founding year, enrollment estimate
- Brief culture/identity summary (from public sources)
- Representation status
- Join button + share/QR flow
- Independence disclaimer
